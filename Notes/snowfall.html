Precipitation Type:

Precipitation types that are included in the measuring of snowfall amount:

  • SN, SHSN, ...

Showers (SHSN)

  • Typically produced by cumuliform cloud;
  • Often, but not always, begins and ends abruptly - typically in short periods, however, can last for much longer - usually with rapid fluctuations in intensity;

Continuous (SN)

  • Typically produced by stratiform cloud;
  • Usually associated with the approach of a warm front;
  • Can be both, continuous OR intermittent;
    • Considered continuous if it continues without a break for at least one hour preceding the time of observation OR continues without a break since beginning within the hour preceding the observation;
    • Precipitation that is not showery in nature is considered intermittent when it has stopped and recommenced at least once within the hour preceding the observation;

Intensity:

  • The intensity of snowfall SHALL be determined by visibility;
  • This applies to: SN, SHSN, SG, GS, DZ, FZDZ;
    • LGT : Visibility of 5/8 or more;
    • MDT : If alone AND Visibility of 1/2 or 3/8;
    • HVY : If alone AND visibility of 0, 1/8, or 1/4;
  • * Alone: occurring with no other precipitation; OR any other obstructions to vision;


Reporting Snowfall - Metars, Specials and Check Obs

Snowfall Remarks:

/Sss/ (Depth Of Newly Fallen Snow): MANOBS 10.2.19.9 to 10.2.19.9.3

  • Depth of newly fallen snow, since the last main synoptic report;
  • It is measured prior to the transmission of the METAR, only when snowfall is occurring, or has occurred within the hour preceding the METAR;
  • It is taken from the approved weaver board, measured with the snow ruler and rounded to the nearest whole centimeter;
  • /Sss/ SHALL be reported only when the rounded hourly value equals or exceeds 1cm;
  • One half of the weaver board should be cleared each hour in which snow has fallen, while the other half should be cleared at each synoptic observation, when snow has fallen. This ensures accurate measurements are being taken at each hourly and synoptic observation.
  • Remember to keep in mind, melting and wind will effect this amount. If all of the newly fallen snow has melted by the time the METAR is taken, /Sss/ will be omitted;

Reporting Snowfall - Synoptic Observations

Section I

Col. 9: MANOBS 13.3.8

  • 6hr snowfall amount, taken from the ground or approved weaver board, recorded in centimeters and tenths;
  • When there is less than a measurable amount (< 0.2cm), but snow was observed within the current synoptic period, the amount SHALL be recorded as "TR";
  • Lastly, the amount SHALL be recorded as "0" for none;

Col. 10: MANOBS 13.3.9

  • 6hr water equivalent, taken from the Nipher Snow Gauge, recorded in millimeters and tenths using a graduated cylinder;
  • For stations without a snow gauge, the water-equivalent amount SHALL be estimated (snow in cm / 10);

Col. 11: MANOBS 13.3.10

  • 6hr rainfall amount, taken from Type B Rain Gauge, recorded in millimeters and tenths;

Col. 12: MANOBS 13.3.11

  • Total 6hr precipitation amount, all types, recorded in millimeters and tenths;

Col. 13: MANOBS 13.3.12

  • Total 24hr precipitation amount, all types, recorded in millimeters and tenths;

Col. 14: MANOBS 13.3.13

  • Depth of snow on the ground, recorded in whole centimeters, rounded to the nearest;

Section III

4E'sss: MANOBS 12.4.4

  • Depth of snow on the ground, in whole centimeters, and taken from column 14;
  • This group SHALL be recorded at 12z when snow, ice, or any solid precipitation is present on the surface, even in trace amounts;
  • Otherwise, recorded at 18z, 00z, and 06z ONLY when some form of solid precipitation is on the surface AND precipitation, of any kind, has occurred at the station within the current synoptic period;
  • At 18z, 00z, and 06z, if there is solid precipitation of any form present on the surface, however, precipitation has not occurred at the station within the current synoptic period, this group SHALL be omitted

931ss: MANOBS 12.4.11 (Para: 2) and MANOBS 12.4.11.3.5 (Encoding Table)

  • Depth of newly fallen snow for the current synoptic period, prior to any melting, transmitted in whole centimeters (taken from column 9 and rounded to the nearest whole centimeter);
  • Essentially, the accumulative sum of the recorded hourly amounts ('/Sss/' before rounding) within the current synoptic period

1ssss:

  • 24hr Snowfall, in centimeters and tenths, from column 9;

2SwSwSwSw:

  • 24hr Snow Water Equivalent, in millimeters and tenths, from column 10;
